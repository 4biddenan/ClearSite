# GLOBAL WORKSPACE RULES: CLEARSITE SOLUTIONS
## 1. Multi-Tier Service Architecture (The "You vs. We" Partnership)
All code interfaces, calculation nodes, and content copies must cleanly present and support the 4-tier structural operational framework, sorted from premium-expensive to economy-cheap:
LEVEL 1: The "Don't Lift a Finger" Pick-Up (Premium Tier)
    You: Point at the mess. No bagging, boxing, or sorting required.
    We: Supply a 2-man crew to sort, bag, pull nails, extract from inside the property, handle all transport, and absorb private commercial yard tipping/tonnage fees.
LEVEL 2: The Driveway / Curbside Pick-Up (Standard Tier)
    You: Neatly stage all items out at the property line, driveway, or curb before arrival. Lumber must be bundled to manageable lengths.
    We: Execute a rapid loading sequence straight from the street perimeter. Zero vehicle tracking on the client's driveway.
LEVEL 3: The On-Site Disposal Meeting (Economy-Plus Tier)
    You: Meet our vehicle directly at the disposal yard gate (local ecocentre or private facility) to present local residency photo ID + utility bill for a free municipal allocation run, or pay the gate fees with your card.
    We: Perform 100% of the on-site loading at your house independently, handle safe transit, and coordinate the gate meeting point.
LEVEL 4: The Full Co-Pilot Labor Share (Maximum Savings Tier)
    You: Ride along inside the truck cab as a passenger co-pilot to grant municipal gate access, and actively share 1-for-1 physical loading and sorting labor.
    We: Provide the 12-foot utility vehicle, fuel, a professional logistics operator, and match your physical labor output equally.

## 2. Rigorous Operational & Weight Constraints
THE MONDAY CLOSED CONSTRAINT: Municipal ecocentres are strictly CLOSED ON MONDAYS. Any UI booking element or SEO text block handling Level 3 or Level 4 scheduling must visually restrict or warn that these tiers operate Tuesday-Sunday only. Monday pickups must route via Level 1 or Level 2 commercial paths.
STRUCTURAL VEHICLE AXLE CAP: Dense debris (concrete, masonry, bricks, tiles, dirt) cannot structurally exceed 1/4 of total truck volume capacity (maximum 3 m³). If a user pushes inputs past this threshold with heavy aggregate metrics active, the UI must lock checkout paths and mandate multi-trip routing.
ZERO-TOLERANCE CONTAMINANT BAN: Absolutely zero extraction or transit of asbestos, lead paint, volatile chemical canisters, propane tanks, or medical waste. If detected on site, immediate job cancellation protocols trigger.

## 3. Vehicle Operation Safety (Co-Pilot Firewall)
VEHICLE OPERATOR CONTROLS: Under no circumstances may any code or copy asset imply that a customer drives, controls, routes, or handles the steering wheel of the company vehicle.
CO-PILOT ROLE SPECIFICATION: Level 4 is explicitly a passenger-accompaniment role only. Any text block violating this constraint (e.g., "rent our truck", "take the wheel") triggers an automatic compliance failure.

## 4. Frontend & Theme Requirements
STACK BOUNDARIES: Pure semantic HTML5, utility Tailwind CSS, and zero-dependency Vanilla JS.
COLOR TOKENS: Primary Navy Blue (#1A365D), Accent Emerald Green (#10B981), Background Slate Grey (#F1F5F9). No generic unstyled layouts.

## 5. Multi-Device Responsive Layout Rules
- MOBILE-FIRST BOUNDARY: All structural styles must be authored mobile-first. Default classes must target small touchscreens, using `lg:` modifiers exclusively to handle desktop transformations.
- FLUID SCROLLING SAFEGUARD: Horizontal page scrolling is strictly prohibited. Use fluid percentages or viewport segments (`w-full`, `max-w-screen-xl`) to wrap layout boundaries.
- TOUCH TARGET SIZE MINIMUMS: All interactive elements—including range slider thumbs, tab headers, and checkbox labels—must maintain a minimum interactive hit area of 44x44 pixels on viewports below 1024px to satisfy mobile usability criteria.
